A new kind of benchmark that measures your web browser's latency and jank.

Latency and Jank?

Latency and jank represent the responsiveness of a browser's user interface. Common browser benchmarks such as SunSpider or Octane measure raw JavaScript speed, but not UI responsiveness. A browser could be fast at calculating 1,000,000 digits of pi and still slow at responding to your commands.

Here's an example of how latency affects responsiveness. Try dragging the two boxes below.

Drag me.
I have no added latency.

Drag me.
I have 6 frames of added latency.

Latency is measured here in frames, assuming 60 frames per second. 6 frames of added latency means it takes the box an extra 1/10 of a second to respond.Interactive web pages like maps and games need low latency.

Here's an example of jank. Try scrolling the two boxes below.

Scroll me.
I should scroll smoothly.
I have no added jankiness.

Scroll me.
I will be periodically interrupted by 20 frames of jank.

Jank happens when the browser stops updating the screen because it's busy doing something else. 20 frames of jank means everything pauses for 1/3 of a second. Jank can be caused by all sorts of things, including page layout, image loading, and JavaScript.
